A Glimpse into History: AUB Campus in 1955

Beirut.com - AUB 1955

Beirut, Lebanon – A captivating photograph from 1955 offers a nostalgic look at the American University of Beirut (AUB) campus. The image highlights the university’s iconic architecture, lush greenery, and mid-century charm, capturing students strolling through the serene grounds. Founded in 1866, AUB has been a cornerstone of education and cultural exchange in the region. By the 1950s, it was a thriving hub for innovation and academic excellence. This historic snapshot serves as a reminder of the university’s enduring legacy, which continues to shape Lebanon and the Arab world today.

Hamra Street in 1973: A Glimpse into Beirut's Golden Era

Beirut.com - Hamra 1973

Beirut, Lebanon – A newly rediscovered photograph from 1973 offers a vibrant look at Hamra Street during its heyday. Once the beating heart of Beirut, the image captures the street bustling with life, lined with cars of the era and framed by its signature blend of modernist architecture and thriving businesses. In the 1970s, Hamra was a cultural and commercial hub, home to theaters, cafés, and shops that drew locals and tourists alike. It symbolized a cosmopolitan Beirut, renowned as the “Paris of the Middle East.” This historic snapshot not only evokes nostalgia but also serves as a reminder of the city’s resilience and enduring spirit, even as it has transformed over the decades.

Charter and Academic Growth

LAU.com-1946 campus

In 1948–49 the AJCW program was expanded under the name Beirut College for Women (BCW). In 1950, it was granted a provisional charter by the Board of Regents of the University of the State of New York and it was authorized to bestow four-year Bachelor of Arts degrees and two-year associate degrees. In 1955 the Board of Regents granted BCW an absolute charter with all its rights and privileges, including the authority to also hand out Bachelor of Science degrees.
